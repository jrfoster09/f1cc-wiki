F1CC Performance Index

ELO Ratings

Driver ratings built from race results, qualifying, and teammate battles across every F1CC season. Recomputed automatically after each sync.

How ratings are calculated

Every driver starts at 1500. After each race or sprint, their rating shifts across five components: how they finished against the full field, how they qualified against the field, and two head-to-head battles with their teammate (race and qualifying). A car adjustment accounts for machinery: drivers in top constructors need higher finishes to gain rating. Any retirement (DNF, RET, DSQ) carries a flat −5 penalty regardless of cause.

F2 graduates into F1 carry forward a converted starting ELO: 1500 + (F2 final − 1500) × 20%. Between F2 seasons, ratings regress 50% toward 1500 so veterans who struggled in one season aren't permanently disadvantaged against fresh starters. F2 alumni remain on the F2 chart permanently, even after moving to F1.
